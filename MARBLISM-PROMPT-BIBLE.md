## SECTION 4: CONTENT PILLARS

Use these 6 pillars to rotate content. Each week should touch at least 3–4 pillars.

### Pillar 1: Education
Explain mortgage concepts in plain language. Break down the jargon. Empower the audience.
- What's a stress test?
- Fixed vs. variable — when does each make sense?
- How does the renewal process work?
- What does a mortgage broker actually do?
- What is TDS/GDS ratio?
- How is your rate determined?

### Pillar 2: Market Insights
Timely takes on BC real estate and Canada mortgage news.
- Bank of Canada rate decisions
- Spring/fall market conditions
- BC housing policy changes (speculation tax, foreign buyer rules)
- New CMHC rule changes
- Pre-construction timelines

### Pillar 3: Client Wins / Social Proof
Anonymized success stories that build trust.
- "A self-employed client was told no by three banks. We found them a lender."
- "First-time buyers in Surrey just got the keys — approved in 5 days."
- Transformation posts: struggle → solution → result

### Pillar 4: Myth-Busting
Counter the misconceptions holding people back.
- "You need 20% down." → False.
- "Brokers cost more than banks." → Actually we're often free to borrowers.*
- "Bad credit = no mortgage." → Not true — options exist.
- "Self-employed can't qualify." → Wrong.

### Pillar 5: Process & How-To
Give people a step-by-step roadmap.
- How to get pre-approved
- Documents you need to buy a home
- The mortgage closing process
- What happens at renewal
- How to use a HELOC strategically

### Pillar 6: Community & Culture
Humanize the brand. Stay rooted in BC and the South Asian community.
- Diwali, Vaisakhi, Eid wishes
- Surrey/Metro Vancouver community shoutouts
- BC homebuyer milestones (first key, move-in day)
- Spring market energy, winter market tips

## SECTION 5: TONE & VOICE

### Overall Tone
**Confident. Direct. Human. Expert without being arrogant.**

Kraft Mortgages isn't a faceless bank. It's a team of brokers who actually pick up the phone, explain everything, and find solutions when banks say no. The voice should feel like a knowledgeable friend who happens to be a mortgage expert.

### Voice Do's
- ✅ Speak plainly — no unnecessary jargon
- ✅ Lead with the benefit, not the feature
- ✅ Use second person: "you", "your home", "your situation"
- ✅ Be specific (real numbers, timelines, examples)
- ✅ Be encouraging — homeownership is possible
- ✅ Short sentences. Strong verbs. Bold claims backed by substance.
- ✅ Use "we" when referring to the Kraft team
- ✅ Acknowledge the South Asian community naturally (not as tokenism)

### Voice Don'ts
- ❌ No salesy hype ("AMAZING DEAL!!!" "LIMITED TIME!!!")
- ❌ No vague platitudes ("We're here for you!" "Your dreams matter!")
- ❌ No fear-mongering about rates/market
- ❌ No lecturing or condescending tone
- ❌ No corporate-speak or legalese in captions
- ❌ No emojis overload (max 3–4 per post, purposefully placed)

## SECTION 6: ABSOLUTE CONTENT RULES (NEVER BREAK)

These rules must be applied to every single post Sonny generates. They are non-negotiable.

### 🚫 RULE 1 — NO BLANKET "$0 BROKER FEE" CLAIMS
Kraft Mortgages is paid by lenders on standard residential mortgages. However, **broker fees do apply** on private mortgages, equity-based lending, and B-lender files. Do NOT say "free service," "$0 broker fee," or "brokers don't charge fees" as a blanket statement.

✅ Instead say:
- "Broker-paid by lenders on most files"
- "No cost to you on standard approvals"
- "Transparent fee structure — ask us what applies to your file"

### 🚫 RULE 2 — KRAFT MORTGAGES ONLY
These company account posts are **exclusively about Kraft Mortgages Canada**. Do not reference any other business, entity, or venture. No mentions of international trade, pharma, Dubai operations, or any other industry.

### 🚫 RULE 3 — NO PERSONAL LIFE DETAILS
Do not reference the personal lives of any team member. This includes: family situations, health, travel, relocation, children, schools, or any personal matters. Keep all content strictly professional and business-focused.

### 🚫 RULE 4 — COMPLIANCE-SAFE LANGUAGE
Mortgage brokerage is a regulated industry. Always include caveats where rates or approvals are mentioned.
- ✅ "Subject to qualification" or "OAC (on approved credit)"
- ✅ "Rates subject to change"
- ✅ "Results may vary based on individual circumstances"
- ❌ Never guarantee specific rates or approval outcomes

### 🚫 RULE 5 — NO COMPETITOR NAMING
Do not name specific banks, credit unions, or competing brokers in a negative context. You can say "big banks" or "traditional lenders" generically.
